Brand Foundation

Who we are, what we stand for, and the strategic context behind every design decision.

Brand Statement

Pryes is a Minnesota craft brewery with a beer for everyone.

We're setting the new standard for Minnesota craft beer. Our large variety includes style-defining sours, Midwest IPAs, classic pilsners and more — intentionally brewed and consistently excellent. We're easy to love and even easier to drink. No gimmicks. No guesswork. Just well-made beer you can bring anywhere.

Mission & Vision

Mission: To help every Minnesotan find their favorite beer.

Vision: To be a top 5 Minnesota brewery.

Brand Pillars

Approachable Craft

We take the craft seriously, but we keep the experience easy. From our beer flavor graphs to our friendly taproom service, we make exploring craft beer feel comfortable, not complicated. Good beer shouldn't feel intimidating, and we make sure it never does.

By Minnesotans, For Minnesotans

We are the largest Minnesota-only brewery, by design. While others go wide, we go deep: staying here, self-distributing and putting all our energy into making beer this state can be proud of.

A Beer for Everyone

We want everyone to find a beer they love. That's why our lineup spans easy drinkers, hazy IPAs, bright sours, rich stouts, NA options and more. Whatever your taste, there's a Pryes beer for you. And if you're not sure what you like yet, we'll help you find your favorite.

Intentional Quality

Experimentation is part of craft brewing, but it doesn't always lead to something you want to drink twice. We take a steadier approach. We don't chase trends or cut corners — we brew with intention and only release beers that meet our standards. You can always trust that you're getting the highest quality.

Positioning

We're just as ambitious as Surly or Bell's, but in a more Minnesota-centric way. We are more approachable and less niche than other MN craft brands like Modist and Indeed. We're a potential entry point for emerging craft beer drinkers. We're more modern than Summit, but we have potential to be just as timeless.

In the Twin Cities market, we have more variety on shelf and a larger distribution than most craft breweries — local or regional. Pryes is the brewery with a beer for everyone. The enjoy-it-anywhere beer that's never a gamble.

Phase 3 Context

Phase 1 established a cohesive visual identity from scratch. Phase 2 expanded that identity aggressively to boost visibility across packaging, marketing, and distribution. That expansion was necessary and successful, but it also introduced visual fragmentation: too many color treatments, too many typefaces, inconsistent layouts.

Phase 3 is about pulling it all back together. Not starting over — refining. Keep what works, cut what doesn't, lock in rules.

Governing Principles

1
Distinctive at a glance.

Anything we put into the world should read "Pryes" before someone reads the name. Ownable brand cues, consistent across packaging, taproom, retail, OOH, radio, social, and on-premise.

2
Simplify everything.

Say more with less. Reduce visual clutter, reduce messages per piece, and make our work easier to recognize and easier to shop.

3
Unify the system.

Stop visual expansion. Reunite around a tighter set of core assets — logo usage, type, layout, color method, and pattern rules. Remove or merge anything that doesn't fit.

4
Repetition is the strategy.

More Pryes facings on a shelf with one consistent look creates a billboard effect. The brand system stays locked; the product inside changes.

The Blur Test

If you blur any Pryes piece — a can, a poster, a social post, a sell sheet — and the brand is still recognizable from color, shape, and layout alone, the system is working. If it's not recognizable, we haven't been disciplined enough.

The Decision Filter

Before creating or approving any brand asset, ask: Does this pass the blur test? Does it follow the system? Could someone on the team produce this without custom design decisions? If any answer is no, it doesn't ship.

Our Advantages

  • Distribution reach. The largest self-distributed brewery in Minnesota, available in 1,200+ bars, restaurants, and liquor stores.
  • Taproom scale. The second-largest taproom in Minnesota — a massive owned stage for the brand.
  • Variety advantage. 2–4x the product range of many peers. More products on a shelf = more repetitions of the Pryes brand.
  • Proven quality. Beer competition wins and strong reviews. Credibility cues that support but never lead.

Audience

The people most likely to choose Pryes again and again. Understanding them shapes everything from packaging to social posts.

Core Audience

Casual Craft Drinkers. Men and women, roughly 25–60, Twin Cities metro area, upper-middle to high income. They want something craft and local that feels a notch above mass-market brands, but not weird or intimidating. Something solid, no surprises.

They look for approachable beer that fits seamlessly into casual gatherings, family dinners, happy hour. Most are novice to intermediate craft drinkers — still figuring out what they like, or have basic preferences ("I know I like IPAs").

Why They Choose Pryes

  • A trusted friend or pro recommended it
  • Brand and packaging feel approachable, elevated, and helpful in guiding expectations
  • In stores, the broad, well-stocked shelf signals legitimacy and reliability
  • "By MN, for MN" resonates — it feels like the quality Minnesota choice, not the small local compromise
  • They can be proud to bring it anywhere

Typography System

Two fonts with two distinct jobs, rooted in a phrase that has guided the brand from the beginning.

The Philosophy

The Pryes type system is built on the idea: “Masterfully crafted, thoughtfully designed.”

Masterfully Crafted = Instrument Sans. The structure, the system, the consistency. The backbone that makes everything work.

Thoughtfully Designed = Instrument Serif. The moments of elegance and personality. Beer names, the tagline, the numbers that describe what's in the glass.

These two fonts were designed as a family. They share proportions, x-height, and rhythm, which means they work together without forcing a pairing. The cohesion is built in.

The Fonts

Instrument Sans Foundation · “Masterfully Crafted”
MASTERFULLY
Crafted
Weights: 400–700
Widths: 75%–100%
License: Open source (Google Fonts)
Variable: Yes

Use For

  • The PRYES wordmark
  • Style descriptors (Midwest IPA, Classic Pils, Sour Ale)
  • Tier markers (Signature Series, Seasonal, etc.)
  • All body copy, descriptive text, and supporting information
  • Menus, sell sheet text, signage, legal
  • Social media captions and CTAs
  • Anything at small sizes

Why sans for the wordmark? PRYES is the foundation of everything. It's the structural anchor on every can, sign, sell sheet, and menu. The wordmark is the craft — it's “masterfully crafted.”

Rule

If you're unsure which font to use, use Instrument Sans. It is always the safe, correct choice.

Instrument Serif Accent · “Thoughtfully Designed”
Miraculum
Trust your taste.
Weight: 400 (Regular)
Style: Roman only (no italic)
License: Open source (Google Fonts)
Sizes: Display only

Use For

  • Beer names (Miraculum, Pragmatic, Royal Raspberry, Main Squeeze)
  • “Trust your taste.” tagline
  • Numbers and stats (ABV percentages, IBU, FL OZ — the numbers themselves, not labels)

Why serif for beer names? PRYES in sans says “this is who we are.” Miraculum in serif says “this is what we made.” The brand is the craft; the beer is the design.

Rule

Instrument Serif appears at display sizes only. Never use it for body copy, small supporting text, or anything that requires extended reading. No italics — legibility was Heritage's problem; we don't repeat it.

Instrument Sans Condensed Utility Width

Width: 75%
Not a separate font: Same family, no extra licensing

Purpose: Space recovery. Use the condensed width anywhere space is at a minimum but legibility or visibility still needs to be prioritized.

Rule

Use sparingly. Condensed is a problem-solving tool for real space constraints, not a stylistic alternative. Never use condensed for headlines, beer names, the wordmark, or the tagline. Never mix condensed and standard widths in the same text block.

Color System

The master palette, SKU color logic, and rules for cohesion across the lineup.

The Approach

Color is the single most powerful tool for shelf recognition. Phase 3 introduces palette cohesion: every SKU color must feel like it belongs to the same family. When five Pryes cans sit side by side, the colors should be obviously different from each other but obviously related.

Brand Colors

Pryes Black
#1A1A1A
Primary brand color. Backgrounds, wordmark, headers.
Pryes Gold
#C5A55A
Accent and highlight. Dividers, labels, tier markers.
Pryes Cream
#F5F3EE
Light backgrounds, secondary surfaces, text on dark.

SKU Color Rules

  • Depth and richness. Muted and grounded, not neon or pastel. Colors you'd find in nature, a well-aged library, or a quality textile.
  • Consistent saturation. All SKU colors sit at a similar saturation level so they feel related side by side.
  • Sufficient contrast. Each SKU color must be clearly distinguishable from every other at a glance.
  • White and cream elements must work on every color. Logo, wordmark, and beer name appear in light tones on every can.
Decide
A master palette with defined hue ranges, saturation, and value consistency for all current and future SKUs.
Define
A process for adding new SKU colors. Include contrast testing and family cohesion checks.
Eliminate
Any current SKU colors that clash with the family, duplicate another SKU, or don't support legible white/cream elements.

Product Tier Architecture

The four product tiers, how beers move between them, and how each tier appears in the brand system.

The Pipeline

Pryes operates a pipeline where beers develop upward through tiers based on performance and demand. Small Batch beers that prove themselves get revised and promoted to Seasonal. Seasonals that earn sustained demand can become Signature flagships.

Signature
Year-round flagships. Highest visibility, most shelf facings, most marketing investment. Full system compliance, maximum consistency.
Seasonal
Rotating releases tied to a season or occasion. Same core layout and brand cues as Signature, with a clear seasonal marker.
Collaboration
Co-branded beers with partners. Pryes brand cues remain recognizable, with defined flexibility zones. Must still pass the blur test.
Taproom Small Batch
Experimental and limited beers available only at the taproom. The R&D tier. Simplified system for crowler and draft-only formats.

Tier Signaling

  • Present on every piece of packaging in the same position
  • Readable but not dominant — it's information, not the headline
  • Consistent in typography (Instrument Sans, small caps, tracked out)
  • Distinct enough for bartenders and liquor store staff to quickly sort and identify

Layout & Packaging

The locked layout template, what flexes, what doesn't, and how it works across every format.

The Locked Template

Every can, box, bottle, and crowler shares the same bones — same zones for logo, beer name, style descriptor, tier marker, and legal info. The layout does not change between beers. Only the content inside the zones changes.

What Flexes

  • SKU color. Each beer gets its own color from the master palette.
  • Beer name. Same zone, same scale, Instrument Serif.
  • Style descriptor. Same position, Instrument Sans.
  • Tier marker. Consistent signal in Instrument Sans.
  • Numbers. ABV, IBU, stats. Instrument Serif, same position.

What Does Not Flex

  • Logo mark, position, and scale
  • PRYES wordmark in Instrument Sans
  • Layout grid and zone structure
  • Typeface selections and hierarchy
  • Color application method

Formats

  • 16oz cans — primary retail and taproom format
  • 16oz can boxes — retail multi-pack format
  • 12oz cans — variety pack and retail format
  • 12oz can boxes — retail multi-pack format
  • Variety pack boxes — 12oz cans in branded boxes
  • Bottles — select products
  • Crowlers — taproom-only, simplified system treatment
Decide
A single master layout grid that adapts across all six formats while maintaining identical zone structure.
Define
Fixed zones: logo position, beer name zone, style and tier zone, stats zone, legal zone. Exact sizes per format.
Eliminate
Ad hoc layouts, one-off compositions, and any packaging that doesn't share the common structure.

Logo System

The consolidated mark set, usage rules, and what gets retired.

Consolidation

Phase 2 expanded logo variations — Phase 3 reduces them. Consolidate to a defined, limited set of marks with clear usage rules.

Decide
Which marks carry forward as primary (packaging) and secondary (supporting). Evaluate the P monogram, coin logo, full wordmark, and laurel wreath.
Define
Minimum size, clear space, and placement rules per format. Define which backgrounds each mark works on.
Eliminate
Redundant or inconsistent logo variations from Phase 2. If two marks do the same job, one goes.

Patterns & Texture

Evaluate the role of patterns and texture. Phase 3 should either lock in one consistent pattern language or remove patterns entirely if they don't serve recognition.

Voice & Messaging

How Pryes sounds in every context — from a can label to an Instagram caption to a sales conversation.

The Platform

External platform: “Trust Your Taste.” The long-term message that gives consumers permission to choose Pryes regardless of style preference. Appears in Instrument Serif — it's the voice of thoughtful design speaking directly to the customer.

Internal mantra: “Find your favorite.” Guides how we talk about the lineup internally. Reinforces variety, discovery, and the idea that there's a Pryes beer for every preference. Appears in Instrument Sans — the structural craft of the brand at work.

Voice

Our brand voice is warm, confident, and refreshingly down-to-earth. We take brewing seriously, but we don't take ourselves too seriously. We know our craft deeply, but we talk like humans, not beer textbooks — clear, familiar language that makes everyone feel welcome.

Our Voice Is
relaxed confident warm welcoming down-to-earth self-aware lightly witty knowledgeable clear
Our Voice Is Not
exclusive trendy pretentious corporate loud goofy abstract gimmicky edgy mysterious
We sound like:

A knowledgeable friend who recommends the right beer without making you feel like you ordered the wrong one.

We don't sound like:

A beer snob lecturing you on hop profiles. Or a party brand shouting for attention.

Tone by Channel

Our voice stays constant. Tone flexes by context:

WebsiteConfident, clear
Social mediaPlayful, conversational
Sales materialsPolished, trustworthy, credible
Customer serviceCalm, empathetic
CampaignsBold, expressive, catchy

Writing Guidelines

  • Keep it simple. Use active voice, familiar words. Get to the point fast.
  • Include everyone. Follow inclusive language and accessibility standards. Keep pop culture references and generational slang to a minimum.
  • Use punctuation well. Oxford comma. Exclamation points and ALL CAPS sparingly — we rarely get that excited. Use the right dash for the right purpose.
  • Emojis in social only. Don't use them to replace a word. One is almost always enough.

Minnesota Voice

We are unapologetically Minnesotan, but we approach regional clichés with care. We would post old photos from the Halloween Blizzard of '91. We would reference a Minnesota goodbye. We would post beers in a garage fridge or snowbank. We would maybe say "ope." We would never say "uff dah," "dontcha know," or "you betcha." We do not assume everyone is into hunting, ice fishing, hockey, football, corn, and flannel.

Messaging Hierarchy

  1. Brand first. The Pryes name and visual identity are the most prominent element.
  2. Beer second. The beer name and what it is.
  3. Proof third. Awards, reviews, stats. Support, never lead.
  4. Call to action last. Where to find it, what to do next.
One piece, one job.

Every piece of communication should have one primary message. A can sells the beer. A sell sheet closes the deal. A social post drives a single action. If you're trying to say two things, make two pieces.

Content Strategy

Channel roles, content pillars, and the framework that keeps Pryes showing up clearly and consistently everywhere.

2026 Goals

  • Strengthen brand perception. Create a clear, consistent identity that people recognize and trust.
  • Grow awareness. Use sponsorships, partnerships, and owned channels to drive awareness that translates into wholesale growth and taproom traffic.
  • Build scalable, repeatable marketing systems. Move from reactive, one-off execution to clear, repeatable programs.

Content Pillars

Content pillars guide what we create — each one reinforcing our brand pillars and keeping the story consistent across channels.

The Beer

Helps people get familiar with our beers and builds trust. New releases, seasonal features, beer families, variety packs, simple education ("If you like X, try Y"). Use plain language so choosing a beer feels easy.

The Taproom

Drives taproom traffic. What's on tap, event highlights, promos, behind-the-scenes moments, seasonal experiences (patio season, winter sauna, PryesFest), private events and hosting.

Pryes in Real Life

Shows how Pryes fits into real routines and occasions. Supports retail and on-premise purchase. Fridge shots, hosting, game night, backyard hangs, BBQs, cabin weekends. Real and relatable, not overly staged.

Culture & Community

Builds emotional connection, local pride, and loyalty. Local collaborations, team spotlights, Minnesota culture tie-ins (Twins, State Fair, seasons), community events and causes, milestones.

Applications & Deliverables

How the system comes together across every touchpoint. This section will link to individual playbooks and templates as they're built.

Packaging

Highest priority. This is where the brand lives on shelves across 1,200+ locations. Every can, box, bottle, and crowler follows the locked template. Designed first; everything else references it.

Sell Sheets

Primary sales tool for retail buyers and on-premise accounts. Clean, scannable. Beer name in Serif, supporting info in Sans. Stats in Serif.

Taproom Menus

The taproom's packaging — what people scan before choosing. Beer names in Serif, styles and descriptions in Sans, ABV in Serif.

Social Media

Templates, not one-offs. Every post from a defined template set. Tagline in Serif, product posts with Serif beer names and Sans for everything else.

Email

Renovated design system in Figma. Clear hero block, primary CTA above the fold, brand story woven in. Welcome flow for new subscribers.

Website

Shift from taproom-first to product/brand-first. Beer detail pages, mobile-first menus, homepage hero for seasonal highlights. FAQ sections on key pages.

Owned Events

Branded events design playbook. Core graphic system across all events with consistent brand elements. Flexible color and imagery per event.

Signage & OOH

Billboards, tap handles, bar signage, retail displays. The system scales up cleanly. At large scale, the two-font contrast becomes even more effective.

Merch

T-shirts, hats, hoodies, glassware. Logo and wordmark follow the same rules as every other format. Merch is brand advertising.

Sponsorship Activations

Standardized playbook for Twins and other partnerships. Core activation formats reusable year over year. Retail conversion paths beyond the taproom.
